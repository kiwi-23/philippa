Philippa has been trained on multiple moral dilemmas that pit 5 lives against 1. Even though the scenarios all bear the same consequences, our moral intuition will tell us that they are inherently different from one another. That is because there are more moral variables at play than just the number of lives.

A machine usually only understands arithmatic comparisions, 5 is greater than 1, yet Philippa works on a framework that accounts for other moral variables- desire, intent and duty. The framework looks like this-

action[(number),(desire),(intent),(duty)]

For example, for the first and second scenarios, it is one thing to steer towards someone foreseeing that you will kill him and another to aim at his death as part of your plan. There’s no certainty that the worker will die, it is possible that he may find a foothold in the tunnel to cling onto till the train passes. The driver does not jump out of the trolley to kill the worker. The surgeon, however, needs the death of the healthy man for his purposes.

This is the distinction between direct and oblique intent. As the trolley driver, you do not directly intend the person’s death, but as the transplant surgeon, the person’s death is necessary for you to save the five patients.

Philippa

Philippa is a neural network that is capable of measuring the morality of an action through logic, she makes ethical decisions by evaluating the worth of a person's life beyond a simple quantitative (5 > 1) comparison.

You are a trolley driver, and you notice 5 construction workers working towards the end of the track. Your brakes are faulty. However, you notice a side-track where there is only 1 worker. Which is the more ethical course of action? Steer the trolley & kill 1 worker? Do nothing & kill 5 workers?

        

You are a transplant surgeon, you have 5 patients in critical need of an organ each. One needs a liver, two need a kidney, one a heart, another a lung. There are no donors. But there is a healthy patient in the next room. Which is the more ethical course of action? Use the healthy patient's organs to save 5 lives? Do nothing & let the 5 die?

        

You are a judge, and 5 activists are threatening to kill themselves if the culprit of a certain crime is not found. The real culprit remains unknown. You can prevent the bloodshed by framing an innocent individual. Which is the more ethical course of action? Execute the innocent person & save 5 lives? Do nothing & let the 5 die?

        

You are a doctor, and in order to save a patient, you need to give him a massive dose of a drug that's in short supply. A little later, 5 other patients arrive, each of whom could be saved with 1/5th of that dose. Which is the more ethical option? Give the dose to the 5 later patients? Give the dose to the initial patient?

        

You are a doctor, and there are 5 patients whose lives could be saved by the manufacture of a certain gas. But this leads to the release of lethal fumes into the room of another patient who, for some reason, you cannot move elsewhere. Which is the more ethical option? Manufacture the gas & let the 1 patient die? Do nothing & let the 5 patients die?
